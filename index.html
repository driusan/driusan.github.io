I am a software developer who works for Alan Evans' lab at McGill University, where I mostly work on the Loris database for neuroinformatics. In particular, I rewrote their data querying tool (link may point to a private github repo until I can convince management to open source it) as a CouchApp based on CouchDB using HTML5 and javascript. I had a poster about it and how NoSQL was more efficient than MySQL for this purpose at the Organization for Human Brain Mapping 2014 conference.

I've discovered Jekyll and after 15 years of software development specializing in webapps, decided to use it for making a personal website, because it reminds me of simpler times and I'm starting to think this whole internet thing just might take off.
